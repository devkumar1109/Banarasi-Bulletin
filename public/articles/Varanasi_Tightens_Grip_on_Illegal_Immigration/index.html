Varanasi Tightens Grip on Illegal Immigration

2025-04-30T19:34:00+00:00

The Varanasi police have launched a district-wide drive to identify and remove illegal Bangladeshi and Rohingya immigrants. Police commissioner Mohit Agarwal directed increased vigilance at public places like railway stations, bus stands, and temples. He instructed authorities to verify individuals residing on roadsides, railway tracks, and other public areas. Agarwal also emphasized the need for increased CCTV surveillance in rural areas and urged residents to install cameras. He directed a crackdown on drug peddling, cattle smuggling, illegal arms trafficking, and online gambling. The police will conduct regular surprise inspections and increase night patrols. Operation Chakravyuh will involve special checking drives at key locations.
