Uttar Pradesh Sows Seeds of Gene Editing for Growth

2025-05-22T11:41:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh is embracing gene editing technology to boost agricultural growth. A recent workshop at Acharya Narendra Deva University of Agriculture and Technology (ANDUAT) highlighted the potential of genetically modified and gene-edited crops for sustainable agriculture. Dr. K V Raju, Advisor to the Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh, emphasized the need for scientific innovations to address productivity gaps in crops like rice and wheat. The workshop, organized by Biotech Consortium India Limited (BCIL) and the Federation of Seed Industry of India (FSII), aimed to raise awareness and promote policy alignment for biotechnology applications in agriculture. Dr. Raju stressed the importance of increasing yield, diversifying crops, and utilizing biotechnology for the state's agricultural advancement.
