90 Birds Freed From Illegal Trade in Varanasi

2025-05-03T18:22:00+00:00

In a joint operation, forest officials and police rescued 90 birds from illegal trade in Varanasi's Baheliya Tola. The rescued birds included 13 Indian Parrots, 40 Red Avadavats, 17 Buntings, 18 Budgerigars, and 2 Java Sparrows. The birds were taken to Sarnath Zoo for medical care and were later released. Varanasi is a sensitive area for wildlife trafficking due to its proximity to forest areas and the Ganga river. Baheliya Tola is known for illegal wildlife trade connected to Bihar, Jharkhand, and West Bengal. Forest officials arrested the culprits and registered a forest crime case.
