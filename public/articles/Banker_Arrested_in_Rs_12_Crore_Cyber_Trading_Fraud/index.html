Banker Arrested in Rs 1.2 Crore Cyber Trading Fraud

2025-04-24T18:33:00+00:00

Hyderabad Cybercrime Police arrested Deepak Kumar, a relationship manager at a private bank in Uttar Pradesh, for his involvement in a Rs 1.2 crore trading fraud case. Kumar allegedly provided a bank account to cyber fraudsters through Telegram for a commission. The account was linked to 23 cybercrime cases nationwide with transactions totaling Rs 6 crore. A Hyderabad resident lost Rs 1.2 crore due to this fraud, leading to the police booking a case and Kumar's arrest.
