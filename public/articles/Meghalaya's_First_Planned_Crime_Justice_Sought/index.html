Meghalaya's First Planned Crime: Justice Sought

2025-06-18T14:14:00+00:00

Meghalaya Minister Alexander Laloo Hek called the Raja Raghuvanshi murder case the state's first organized, pre-planned crime. He emphasized the government's commitment to swift justice and a crime-free Meghalaya. The SIT meticulously reconstructed the crime scene, including revisiting the river site and involving the accused, Sonam. Minister Hek claimed that Sonam and Raja Raghuvanshi's families initially blamed Meghalaya and its people but later apologized. The SIT's crime scene reconstruction was successful, providing a clearer picture of the events. However, one machete used in the murder remains missing. The Meghalaya police are continuing their investigation and aim to file a chargesheet within the stipulated time.
