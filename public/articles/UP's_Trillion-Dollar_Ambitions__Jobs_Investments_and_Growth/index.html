UP's Trillion-Dollar Ambitions: Jobs, Investments, and Growth

2025-04-25T06:57:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh CM Yogi Adityanath aims to transform the state into a trillion-dollar economy by 2029. While UP has moved up to the second position economically among Indian states, its projected GDP in 2024-25 ranks fifth, behind Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Gujarat. The state government claims ₹15 lakh crore of ₹45 lakh crore investment proposals received since 2018 have been implemented, creating 60 lakh jobs. UP's unemployment rate at 2.4% is below the national average. To achieve the trillion-dollar goal, the government plans another Investors' Summit, targeting ₹33 lakh crore investments, aiming for ₹100 lakh crore by 2027. Invest UP offices will be set up in Mumbai, Delhi, and Bangalore. Digital land use changes and a GIS data bank for industrial land are also planned. The Bundelkhand Industrial Development Authority, with ₹5,000 crore allocated, will focus on industrial growth between Jhansi and Kanpur, utilizing 56,000 acres of land.
