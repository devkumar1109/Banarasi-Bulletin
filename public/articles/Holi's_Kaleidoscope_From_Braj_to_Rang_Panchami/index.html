Holi's Kaleidoscope: From Braj to Rang Panchami

2025-03-11T07:32:00+00:00

Holi celebrations across India vary in timing and traditions. While Rang Panchami falls on March 14th, Braj region (Mathura and Vrindavan) and Varanasi are already in full swing. Braj ki Holi, a 40-day festival, began on February 12th and concludes on March 22nd. Highlights include Faag Amantran Utsav in Nandgaon, Lath Maar Holi in Barsana, Rangbhari Ekadashi in Vrindavan, and Raman Reti Utsav in Gokul. ISKCON temples, especially in Vrindavan, hold special Holi celebrations marking the birthday of Chaitanya Mahaprabhu. Indore will host its Rangpanchami Ger Festival on March 19th. Popular Holi destinations like Vrindavan, Pushkar, Shantiniketan, Jaipur, and Hampi are witnessing a surge in travel searches.
