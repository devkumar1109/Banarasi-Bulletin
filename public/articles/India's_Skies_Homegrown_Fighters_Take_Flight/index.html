India's Skies: Homegrown Fighters Take Flight

2025-05-15T18:00:00+00:00

India is projected to achieve self-reliance in domestically manufactured fighter jets within the next few years. Aviation scientist Kota Harinarayana, a key figure in the development of the Tejas LCA, attributes this progress to a robust manufacturing ecosystem and advancements in production technology. The Indian Air Force (IAF) plans to gradually replace its aging MiG-series aircraft, further bolstering the domestic aerospace industry. Harinarayana highlighted India's successful defense capabilities during Operation Sindoor, emphasizing the importance of indigenous defense products. The recent testing of Bhargavastra, a low-cost counter-drone system developed by SADAL, showcases India's growing strength in the sector.
