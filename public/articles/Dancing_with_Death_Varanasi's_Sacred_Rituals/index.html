Dancing with Death: Varanasi's Sacred Rituals

2025-04-05T08:49:00+00:00

In Varanasi, sex workers traditionally dance around burning pyres at Manikarnika Ghat during the three-day Shringar Festival celebrating Mahashmashan Nath. This centuries-old tradition, dating back to the renovation of the temple by Raja Sawai Man Singh in 1585, involves tantric rituals, devotional songs, and prayers. The festival culminates with a grand aarti on the final day, with the women hoping for salvation and a better next life. Flowers and music adorn the ghat, and the event draws crowds despite the smoke and crackling sounds of the pyres.
