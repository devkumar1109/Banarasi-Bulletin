NAM Conclave 2025: Boosting India's Ayush Sector

2025-05-02T16:12:00+00:00

The NAM Conclave 2025 in Lonavala, Maharashtra, focused on boosting India's traditional medicine sector. Day 2 emphasized quality service delivery in Ayush facilities through implementing Indian Public Health Standards (IPHS). Targets for IPHS compliance are 30% by 2026, 40% by 2028, and 50% by 2029. The session also highlighted the importance of medicinal plants in conservation and climate adaptation, and the launch of the Ayush Grid, a nationwide digital health infrastructure. Regulatory mechanisms were discussed, focusing on addressing uneven implementation of standards across states and combating misleading advertisements. Digital surveillance tools, strict licensing protocols, and awareness campaigns were presented as state-level strategies. Finally, the concluding session focused on investment opportunities in the Ayush sector, emphasizing its potential for economic growth.
