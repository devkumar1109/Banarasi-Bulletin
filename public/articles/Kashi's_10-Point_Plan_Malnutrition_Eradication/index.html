Kashi's 10-Point Plan: Malnutrition Eradication

2025-03-10T12:30:00+00:00

In Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, a '10-Point Kashi Strategy' has significantly reduced malnutrition in children. Led by IAS officers S Rajalingam and Himanshu Nagpal, the strategy focused on improving Anganwadi centers, providing nutritional supplements, and establishing mini Nutritional Rehabilitation Centres (NRCs). Between 2019 and 2022, the district saw a dramatic decrease in Severe Acute Malnutrition (SAM) from 24,820 to 316 cases. The initiative involved renovating and constructing Anganwadi centers, ensuring they were accessible and inviting for children. Additionally, a nutritional program called 'Abhinav Pehel' provided daily iron and multivitamin supplements, millet-based snacks, and fortified foods to children and mothers, enhancing their nutritional intake.
