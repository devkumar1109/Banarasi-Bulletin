Lucknow to Launch BrahMos Missile Production Hub

2025-05-09T02:33:00+00:00

A BrahMos missile manufacturing unit, worth Rs 300 crore, will be inaugurated in Lucknow on May 11 by Defence Minister Rajnath Singh. The unit, a joint venture between DRDO and NPO Mashinostroyeniya, aims to produce 80-100 missiles annually. This facility is part of the Defence Industrial Corridor announced in 2018, with Uttar Pradesh as its centre. Around 1,600 hectares have been allocated for the corridor, attracting major companies and an MoU with BHEL is in discussion. The Lucknow node, encompassing 117 hectares, includes Aerolloy Technologies, whose products have been used in Chandrayaan and fighter aircraft. The corridor has six nodes across UP, with the second phase of land allocation set to launch in Aligarh.
