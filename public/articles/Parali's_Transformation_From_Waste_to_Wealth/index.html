Parali's Transformation: From Waste to Wealth

2025-05-07T12:17:00+00:00

India produces 500 million tons of crop residue annually, primarily rice and wheat straw. Traditionally, farmers burn this residue (parali), causing air pollution, health issues, and harming soil. However, new technology and sustainable practices are highlighting parali's potential as a valuable resource. Proper management can generate extra income for farmers and promote sustainable farming. Government subsidies are encouraging farmers to adopt these innovative approaches, transforming parali from waste to a valuable asset for both farmers and the environment.
