Uttar Pradesh: Cows Fuel Sustainable Farming Boom

2025-03-16T05:30:00+00:00

The Uttar Pradesh government aims to make cow shelters self-reliant by utilizing cow dung and urine for natural farming practices. The state will produce vermicompost from cow dung and sell it to farmers, promoting sustainable agriculture and improving soil fertility. The Animal Husbandry Department will train farmers and shelter staff in fodder production and preservation to enhance cattle nutrition. The government is also investing heavily in stray cattle protection, with a budget allocation of Rs 2,000 crore. This includes the construction of 543 new cow shelters and increased funding per shelter. The government is encouraging livestock farming and supporting cattle rearers through various initiatives.
