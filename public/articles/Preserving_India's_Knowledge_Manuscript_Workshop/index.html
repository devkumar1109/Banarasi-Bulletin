Preserving India's Knowledge: Manuscript Workshop

2025-03-24T18:02:00+00:00

A five-day workshop on manuscript preservation began at the Sampurnanand Sanskrit University (SSU) in Varanasi. Organized by SSU and Gyan Bharati (National Manuscript Mission), the workshop aims to preserve approximately 96,000 rare manuscripts. University Vice-Chancellor Professor Bihari Lal Sharma emphasized the importance of preserving these manuscripts for Indian culture and knowledge, likening it to "devoted worship to Mother India." Registrar Rakesh Kumar highlighted the need to make these manuscripts accessible to the public through digital technology and research. The government is providing financial assistance for this project.
