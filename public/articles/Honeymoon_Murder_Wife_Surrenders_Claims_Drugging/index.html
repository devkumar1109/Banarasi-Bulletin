Honeymoon Murder: Wife Surrenders, Claims Drugging

2025-06-10T08:11:00+00:00

Sonam Raghuvanshi, accused of murdering her husband Raja during their honeymoon in Meghalaya, surrendered to UP police. She claimed she was drugged and taken to Ghazipur, attempting to portray herself as a victim. Raja, 30, was found dead in a gorge on June 2nd. Four people, including Raj Kushwaha, an accountant who worked at Sonam's office, have been arrested. Raj's family vehemently denies his involvement, stating he was in Indore at the time of the murder. His sister and mother plead for his innocence, claiming he has been falsely accused.
