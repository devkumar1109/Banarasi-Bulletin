UP Expands Schools for Underprivileged Kids

2025-03-24T13:26:00+00:00

The Uttar Pradesh government is expanding Jai Prakash Narayan Sarvodaya Vidyalayas to 120 by 2024-25, up from 93 in 2017. These schools aim to provide quality education to underprivileged children. Currently, 100 schools are operational, with 70 for boys and 30 for girls, each accommodating 490 students. The government is upgrading the schools with modern facilities like smart classrooms, computer labs, and libraries. Teaching standards are being improved through qualified educators and orientation programs. Admissions are based on a reservation system, with 60% for Scheduled Castes, 25% for Other Backward Classes, and 15% for the General category. Students from families with an annual income of up to Rs 46,080 (rural) or Rs 56,460 (urban) are eligible. The government has allocated Rs 363.91 crore for this initiative.
