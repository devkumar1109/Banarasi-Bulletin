Varanasi Sewai: A Bridge of Flavor and Faith

2025-03-29T19:22:00+00:00

In Varanasi, Hindu artisans have a long tradition of making sewai (vermicelli) for Muslim Eid celebrations. This practice is a testament to cultural integration. ‘Kimami’ sewai, both raw and white varieties, is popular during Eid, while thicker, ghee-cooked ‘soot-pheni’ is favored for 'sehri' during Ramadan. Around 200 families in Varanasi, involving 2,000 people, produce sewai year-round, with production increasing two to three months before Ramadan. Banarasi sewai, known for its thinness and cleanliness, is exported to other countries and is particularly sought after during Eid.
