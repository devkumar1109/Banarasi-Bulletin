Varanasi Sewai: Taste of Eid, Beloved by All

2025-03-29T19:22:00+00:00

In Varanasi, Hindu artisans have a long-standing tradition of manufacturing sewai (vermicelli), a staple food during Muslim Eid celebrations. The 'kimami' sewai, particularly the thin varieties, is highly sought after during Eid. Production involves over 2,000 people across 200 families, utilizing both hand-operated and modern machines. The industry sees a surge in activity before Ramzan, with the demand for Banarasi sewai extending to other countries through exporters. The fine texture and cleanliness of Varanasi's sewai are notable features, making it popular among all communities. Sales peak during Eid, but the product is also in demand during Holi and Bakrid.
