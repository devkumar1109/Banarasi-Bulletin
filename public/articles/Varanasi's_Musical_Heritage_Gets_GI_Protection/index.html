Varanasi's Musical Heritage Gets GI Protection

2025-04-26T19:10:00+00:00

Varanasi, known for its musical heritage, celebrated World Intellectual Property Day 2025 with both Banaras Shehnai and Banaras Tabla holding Geographical Indication (GI) tags. The GI tag ensures only authorized users residing in Varanasi can use these names, protecting the unique cultural identity of these instruments. The Banaras Shehnai, popularized by legendary maestro Ustad Bismillah Khan, is a double-reed woodwind instrument integral to Varanasi's cultural landscape. The Banaras Tabla, crafted from various woods like sheesham and neem, is another cherished instrument with roots in the city. Renowned tabla player Pandit Kishan Maharaj belonged to the Banaras gharana of Hindustani classical music. The Bharat Ratna Ustad Bismillah Khan Foundation and Subah-E-Banaras Anand Kanan spearheaded the applications for the GI tags, respectively. Padma Shri Rajanikant, known as the ‘GI Man of India', played a crucial role in securing these tags with support from NABARD.
