Varanasi Scorches, Assi Ghat Fades in Heatwave

2025-06-13T06:49:00+00:00

A severe heatwave in North India, with temperatures reaching 44 degrees Celsius, has emptied Varanasi's usually bustling Assi Ghat. Tourists are retreating to hotels by 11 am to escape the intense heat, disrupting their sightseeing plans. Local residents are also struggling with the sweltering conditions, experiencing sleepless nights due to the hot winds. The Meteorological Department predicts a weather change after June 13 with the monsoon expected to become active, potentially ending the heatwave. Meanwhile, the Ganga water level in Varanasi is declining, exposing sandbanks, raising concerns about the water security of 45 crore people dependent on the Ganga basin.
