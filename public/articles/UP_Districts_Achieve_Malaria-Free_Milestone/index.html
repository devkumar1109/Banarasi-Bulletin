UP Districts Achieve Malaria-Free Milestone

2025-04-25T19:55:00+00:00

Three Uttar Pradesh districts - Azamgarh, Saharanpur, and Mainpuri - have not reported any indigenous malaria cases since 2022. Chitrakoot has been malaria-free for the past two years. In 2024, eight districts recorded zero malaria cases: Banda, Mahoba, Jalaun, Lalitpur, Deoria, Sant Kabir Nagar, Balrampur, and Rae Bareli. India aims to eradicate malaria by 2030. Uttar Pradesh saw a decrease in malaria cases from 13,603 in 2023 to 13,477 in 2024. No malaria-related deaths were recorded in the past two years. The top ten districts with the highest number of cases were Badaun, Bareilly, Hardoi, Shahjahanpur, Sitapur, Lakhimpur Kheri, Lucknow, Pilibhit, Sonbhadra, and Kanpur Dehat. The state has intensified malaria testing, with 1.45 crore tests conducted in 2024, compared to 1.06 crore in 2023.
