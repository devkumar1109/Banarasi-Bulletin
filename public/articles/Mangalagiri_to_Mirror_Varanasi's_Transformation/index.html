Mangalagiri to Mirror Varanasi's Transformation

2025-03-14T17:11:00+00:00

Andhra Pradesh HRD and IT Minister Nara Lokesh announced plans to develop Mangalagiri on the lines of Varanasi. He aims to model development projects after those initiated at Sri Kasi Viswanath temple in Varanasi and secure funding through the Centre's Prasad scheme. Lokesh highlighted over 100 developmental projects launched in the constituency since his victory, promising modern parks, play areas, widened roads, and improved traffic management within 8-12 months. He also pledged to resolve drainage, drinking water, and LPG connection issues by May. The government intends to grant house sites to 5,000 poor people post-Ugadi festival, including those residing on government and railway lands. Lokesh credited Union Minister Chandrasekhar for securing a railway overbridge at Nidamarru gate and announced plans for a 4-lane road and retaining wall in Sitanagaram. He also inaugurated a modernized Sri Bhagwan Mahavir Gaushala and unveiled a new park.
