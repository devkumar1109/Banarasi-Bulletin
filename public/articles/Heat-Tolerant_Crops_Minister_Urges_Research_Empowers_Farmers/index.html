Heat-Tolerant Crops: Minister Urges Research, Empowers Farmers

2025-06-11T23:06:00+00:00

Union Minister of State for Agriculture Ramnath Thakur urged scientists to develop heat-tolerant crop varieties during a farmers' dialogue program organized by the Indian Institute of Vegetable Research (IIVR). Thakur emphasized the importance of research focused on farmer welfare and encouraged the adoption of new technologies. He inspected a demonstration of direct seeded rice (DSR), a water-saving method, and honored five farmers. The minister also provided Digital Soil Health Cards to ten farmers. IIVR scientists, in collaboration with KVKs and the state agriculture department, have engaged with over 52,000 farmers across six districts, addressing concerns including crop damage, water scarcity, pest management, market access, and seed availability.
