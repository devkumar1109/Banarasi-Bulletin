Uttar Pradesh: India's New Aviation Powerhouse

2025-03-19T07:15:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh will soon be the only state in India with five international airports. The upcoming Noida International Airport (NIA) at Jewar will be the fifth, solidifying the state's position as a major aviation hub. NIA is expected to become India's largest airport, offering modern facilities and boosting regional connectivity. Before 2012, Uttar Pradesh had only two international airports: Lucknow's Chaudhary Charan Singh International Airport and Varanasi's Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport. The state added Kushinagar International Airport in 2021 and Ayodhya International Airport in 2023. NIA, located 72 km from Indira Gandhi International Airport (IGI), will ease congestion in the Delhi-NCR region.
