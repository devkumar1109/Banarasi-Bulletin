Varanasi Crackdown: Targeting Illegals, Strengthening Security

2025-04-30T19:34:00+00:00

Varanasi police, under the direction of Police Commissioner Mohit Agarwal, launched a drive to identify and remove illegal immigrants, particularly Bangladeshis and Rohingyas, from the district. Agarwal directed increased vigilance at public places like railway stations, bus stands, and temples, and instructed authorities to verify the credentials of suspicious individuals. He emphasized tenant verification and urged house owners not to rent property without it. Agarwal also called for enhanced CCTV surveillance in rural areas and requested village heads to promote the installation of cameras. Other directives included cracking down on drug peddling, cattle smuggling, illegal arms trafficking, and online gambling. Police stations were instructed to conduct surprise inspections and increase night patrols.
