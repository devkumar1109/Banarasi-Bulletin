India's Breast Cancer Screening Gap: A Silent Crisis

2025-04-04T02:50:00+00:00

Only 1% of Indian women aged 45 and above undergo breast cancer screening through mammography, significantly lower than global averages (4.5% in Africa, 40-60% in Korea and Japan, and 84% in Europe and America). Kerala has the highest mammography rate at 4.5%, while Nagaland reports zero uptake. Andhra Pradesh and Uttarakhand follow with the lowest rates at 0.1% and 0.27% respectively. The study, published in BioMed Central (BMC) Public Health, analyzed data from over 35,000 women and highlights inadequate coverage of breast cancer early detection under India's National Programme for Prevention and Control of Cancer, Diabetes, Cardiovascular Diseases and Stroke (NPCDCS).
