Kerala Reels: Violence, Drugs Grip State Assembly

2025-03-03T19:30:00+00:00

Kerala assembly debated rising violence and drug crimes. Opposition criticized the government's failure to curb these issues, citing examples like the murder of a Class X student and the surge in synthetic drug use. Congress leader Ramesh Chennithala likened Kerala to Colombia, highlighting the state's growing drug problem. Opposition leader V D Satheesan demanded a stronger enforcement strategy, including dedicated IGs to tackle drug cartels. CM Pinarayi Vijayan attributed the violence to a global trend and alleged that movies and TV serials glorify violence, citing the film 'Avesham' as an example. He questioned the censor board's role in regulating such content. Vijayan emphasized the government's seriousness in addressing the issue, stating that it requires multi-dimensional analysis.
