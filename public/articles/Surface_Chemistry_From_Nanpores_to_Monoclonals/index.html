Surface Chemistry: From Nanpores to Monoclonals

2025-04-03T09:04:00+00:00

IIT BHU Varanasi's School of Biochemical Engineering hosted a seminar-cum-workshop on "Fundamentals of Surface Chemistry and Colloidal Systems." The event, organized by Prof. Prodyut Dhar and Prof. Sumit Kumar Singh, focused on topics like electrokinetics, nanoparticle characteristics, and monoclonal antibody monitoring. The seminar addressed the basics and instrumentation of electrokinetic phenomena, surface interactions in colloidal systems, and the role of surface charge. A session on analytical platforms for monitoring monoclonal antibody aggregation highlighted techniques used in the pharmaceutical industry to address stability and efficacy concerns. The characteristics of nanoparticles, particularly in medical applications, were explored, emphasizing the impact of surface charge on their interaction with biological systems. Finally, the seminar delved into electrokinetic effects within nanopores, highlighting its potential for advancements in nanotechnology and nanomedicine.
