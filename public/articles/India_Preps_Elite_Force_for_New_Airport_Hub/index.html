India Preps Elite Force for New Airport Hub

2025-05-07T15:46:00+00:00

The Uttar Pradesh government is deploying 131 specially trained police personnel to the Noida International Airport (NIA) in Jewar. The unit consists of 10 inspectors, 42 sub-inspectors, and 62 constables. These officers, all under 50 years old, are graduates proficient in English and computer operations. They are trained by the Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO) in Lucknow, covering immigration crimes, suspect identification, and international crime patterns. The training program, led by IPS officer Harsh Singh, is expected to conclude in July. The deployment aims to strengthen NIA's security and immigration framework as it prepares to become a major international gateway.
