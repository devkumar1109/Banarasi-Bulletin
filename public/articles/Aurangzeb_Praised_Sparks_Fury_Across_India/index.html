Aurangzeb Praised, Sparks Fury Across India

2025-03-06T08:48:00+00:00

Aurangzeb, the sixth Mughal emperor, is considered the most hated ruler in Indian history. He reigned from 1658 to 1707 and is known for his oppressive policies, including forced conversions to Islam and brutal punishments. A recent controversy erupted when Samajwadi Party leader Abu Azmi praised Aurangzeb, leading to his suspension from the Maharashtra Assembly and calls for his arrest from Chief Minister Devendra Fadnavis and Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath. While some Muslim historians view Aurangzeb as a "man of perfection," he is widely condemned for his intolerant and inhumane actions.
