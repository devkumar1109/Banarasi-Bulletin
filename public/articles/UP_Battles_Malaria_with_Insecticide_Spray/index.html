UP Battles Malaria with Insecticide Spray

2025-05-23T16:08:00+00:00

The Uttar Pradesh health department is spraying synthetic pyrethroids in nine districts to combat malaria. This initiative aims to protect over 20 lakh people. The chemical insecticide is effective against the Anopheles culicifacies mosquito, which transmits malaria and is prevalent in rural India. The spraying is conducted in areas with an annual parasite incidence (API) greater than one per 1,000 people. Nine districts included in the program are Bareilly, Badaun, Shahjahanpur, Pilibhit, Hardoi, Lakhimpur Kheri, Sitapur, Sambhal, and Sonbhadra. The spraying is expected to be completed by July 31, providing protection for the monsoon season.
