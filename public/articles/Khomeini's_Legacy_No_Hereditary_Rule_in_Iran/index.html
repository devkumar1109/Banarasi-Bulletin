Khomeini's Legacy: No Hereditary Rule in Iran

2025-06-16T11:02:00+00:00

Ayatollah Ruhollah Khomeini, Iran's first Supreme Leader, had a connection to Uttar Pradesh. His grandfather, Ahmad Hindi, was born near Barabanki. Khomeini led the 1979 Islamic Revolution, removing the Western-backed Shah and changing Iran forever. He vehemently opposed hereditary succession, considering it akin to monarchy. This stance is recorded in his writings, "Sahifeyeh Imam Khomeini." Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, the current Supreme Leader, also shares this view. Although Mojtaba, Khamenei's son, is seen as a potential successor by some, Iran's system prevents hereditary rule due to its incompatibility with Islamic principles as defined by Khomeini.
