Wildlife Conservation: Finance for a Thriving Planet

2025-03-03T03:12:00+00:00

World Wildlife Day is observed annually on March 3rd to raise awareness about wildlife conservation. The theme for 2025 is "Wildlife Conservation Finance: Investing in People and Planet." The Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora (CITES) was signed on this day in 1973. CITES is an international agreement between governments aimed at regulating international trade in wild animals and plants to ensure species survival. There are 185 parties to CITES, and the Convention lists species in three Appendices based on the level of protection needed. Appendix I includes species threatened with extinction, with trade permitted rarely under exceptional circumstances.
