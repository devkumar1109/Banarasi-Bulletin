"Health Scandal in Ballia: Fake Nurses and a Rogue Village Head Bring Shame to UP District"

2025-02-23T07:27:00+00:00

Here is a 150-word summary of the news article: In Ballia, Uttar Pradesh, 15 people, including two women, have been accused of fraudulent appointments at health centers. The accused, staff nurses, allegedly submitted forged documents to gain employment and received salaries while misleading the department. A complaint was filed by the District Chief Medical Officer (CMO), Dr. Vijay Pati Dwivedi, which led to an investigation by a seven-member team. The findings prompted the CMO to halt work and salary disbursements to the accused employees, and an FIR has been registered against them. In a separate incident, a village head, Rahul Paswan, has been arrested for allegedly raping a 40-year-old woman in Ballia about a month ago. Paswan was arrested from Ekouni Tiraha area and sent to jail after completing legal proceedings. The case was registered under sections 376 (2)M (punishment for rape) and 351(2) (criminal intimidation) of the IPC.
