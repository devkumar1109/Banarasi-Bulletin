Varanasi Cracks Down on Illegal Immigrants

2025-04-30T19:34:00+00:00

The Varanasi police have launched a district-wide operation to identify and remove illegal Bangladeshi and Rohingya immigrants. Commissioner Mohit Agarwal directed increased vigilance at transportation hubs, religious sites, and public spaces. He ordered the verification of individuals residing in unauthorized areas and urged landlords to conduct tenant checks. Agarwal emphasized the need for enhanced CCTV surveillance, particularly in rural areas, and urged villagers to install cameras. He also called for a crackdown on drug peddling, cattle smuggling, illegal arms trafficking, and online gambling. Hotels, lodges, and eateries will be inspected for illegal activities. The police commissioner directed regular surprise inspections of lodgings and increased night patrols. "Operation Chakravyuh" will focus on major intersections, border routes, and sensitive locations. All vehicles without number plates will be targeted. Agarwal stressed the importance of timely resolution of pending investigations.
