Varanasi Rape: Accused Families Counter-Accuse

2025-04-17T11:08:00+00:00

In the Varanasi gangrape case, the families of the accused are counterclaiming, alleging the victim extorted money from them and shared compromising videos as proof. They presented videos of the woman in "questionable postures" at various locations between March 29 and April 4. The families also claim the victim did not report the alleged rapes to the police earlier. A 6-member SIT, led by a DCP rank officer, has been formed to investigate the case. An FIR was filed on April 6, alleging that a 19-year-old woman was drugged and raped by 23 individuals between March 29 and April 4. 14 people have been arrested so far, including Raj Khan, who was taken into custody on Tuesday.
