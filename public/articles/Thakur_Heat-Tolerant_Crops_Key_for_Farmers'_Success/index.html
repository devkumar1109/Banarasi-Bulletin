Thakur: Heat-Tolerant Crops Key for Farmers' Success

2025-06-11T23:06:00+00:00

Union Minister of State for Agriculture, Ramnath Thakur, urged scientists to develop heat-tolerant crop varieties during a farmer dialogue program in Varanasi. He emphasized the importance of research benefiting farmers and ensuring new technologies reach them. The minister inspected a direct seeded rice (DSR) demonstration at a farmer's field, highlighting its water-saving and production-boosting benefits. Thakur also honored five farmers and distributed Digital Soil Health Cards to 10, aiding soil quality information access. During his visit to the Indian Institute of Vegetable Research (IIVR), Thakur learned about the Krishi Sankalp Abhiyan, where IIVR scientists interacted with over 52,000 farmers across six districts. Farmers raised concerns about nilgai damage, water scarcity, pest management, market access, and seed quality, which the minister directed immediate solutions for.
