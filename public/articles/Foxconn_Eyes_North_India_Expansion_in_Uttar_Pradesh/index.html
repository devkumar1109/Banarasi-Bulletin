Foxconn Eyes North India Expansion in Uttar Pradesh

2025-04-14T16:08:00+00:00

Foxconn, the world's largest iPhone producer, is in talks with the Uttar Pradesh government to establish its first facility in North India. Discussions center around a potential 300-acre plot under the Yamuna Expressway Industrial Development Authority (YEIDA). This move follows the recent allocation of 48 acres for Foxconn's joint venture with HCL, Vama Sundari, which will focus on outsourced semiconductor Assembly and Test (OSAT) with an initial investment of Rs 3,706 crore. While the specific products Foxconn will manufacture in Uttar Pradesh are yet to be determined, the state government is eager to attract the electronics manufacturer.
