UP Expands Schools for Quality Education

2025-03-24T13:26:00+00:00

The Uttar Pradesh government is expanding Jai Prakash Narayan Sarvodaya Vidyalayas to provide quality education to underprivileged children. The number of these schools has increased from 93 in 2017 to 120 by 2024-25. Each school accommodates 490 students, with 70 dedicated to boys and 30 to girls. The government is upgrading these schools with modern facilities like smart classrooms, computer labs, and libraries. Teaching standards are being improved by appointing qualified educators and organizing orientation programs. Admission is free and follows a reservation system, prioritizing economically weaker sections. The government has allocated Rs 363.91 crore to support this initiative, benefiting 32,538 students.
