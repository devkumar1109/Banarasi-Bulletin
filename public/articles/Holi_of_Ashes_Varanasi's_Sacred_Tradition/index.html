Holi of Ashes: Varanasi's Sacred Tradition

2025-03-10T04:41:00+00:00

In Varanasi, Naga Sadhus and Aghoris will celebrate 'Chita Bhasm ki Holi' on March 12th and 13th at Manikarnika and Harishchandra ghats respectively. This unique tradition involves playing with funeral pyre ashes. Organisers condemn attempts to discourage the celebration, emphasizing its long-standing religious significance in Kashi. Aghorpeeth Harishchandra Ghat Kashi's head, Avadhoot Ugra Chandeshwar Kapali, alias Kapali Baba, defends the tradition, stating that Kashi, being Lord Shiva's abode, transcends ordinary rules and celebrates even death. He compares it to other regional Holi celebrations and highlights the religious significance of cremation ashes in Lord Shiva's worship.
