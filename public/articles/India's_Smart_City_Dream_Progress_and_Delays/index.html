India's Smart City Dream: Progress and Delays

2025-04-08T07:27:00+00:00

India's Smart Cities Mission, launched in 2015 to improve 100 cities, faced a March 31, 2025 deadline. Only 18 cities have fully completed their projects, with 7% remaining unfinished. Tamil Nadu and Uttar Pradesh lead with five and four fully implemented cities respectively. Telangana lags behind, with only 64% of its projects finished. Several North Eastern states and other populous states like Bihar, Andhra Pradesh, and Haryana also face delays. The central government allocated Rs 48,000 crore, with states and urban local bodies contributing equally. Private sector partnerships, borrowings, and other mechanisms supplemented the funding. Currently, 559 projects out of 8,063 are ongoing, utilizing Rs 14,239 crore of the total Rs 1.65 trillion allocated. Approximately Rs 60,000 crore was raised through PPPs, bonds, and government contributions.
