Uttar Pradesh's TB-Free Model: Siddharthnagar Sets Example

2025-03-07T10:44:00+00:00

Siddharthnagar district in Uttar Pradesh is a model for TB elimination in India. The district administration implemented a new strategy using ultraportable hand-held X-ray devices for door-to-door screening of high-risk populations, including diabetics, smokers, and those with HIV. This early detection method led to the identification of 2,343 new TB patients. Siddharthnagar has declared 216 gram panchayats TB-free and provides Ni-kshay Poshan kits to diagnosed patients through Ni-kshay Mitras. Over 21,000 people have also received TB preventive treatment. The district government is committed to eliminating TB from Siddharthnagar within a defined timeframe.
