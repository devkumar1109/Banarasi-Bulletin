UP Equips 2,700 Smart Classrooms for Disadvantaged Students

2025-06-13T14:52:00+00:00

The Uttar Pradesh Urban Development Department is implementing a project to establish 2,700 smart classrooms in urban government schools at a cost of Rs 324.56 crore. This initiative aims to provide technology-enabled learning to students from economically disadvantaged backgrounds. 1,780 classrooms are already operational, resulting in increased student enrollment rates. The project aligns with the Smart City Mission and Kayakalp, which focus on improving the quality of education in government schools. Principal Secretary Amrit Abhijat emphasizes the importance of these smart classrooms in preparing students for the digital age.
