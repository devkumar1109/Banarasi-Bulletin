India's March 2: Loss, Legacy, and Lights

2025-03-01T05:19:00+00:00

On March 2, 1949, India lost Sarojini Naidu, known as the Nightingale of India, who was a prominent political activist, poet, and the first woman governor of Uttar Pradesh. This day also marks the birth of legendary music composer Anandji Virji Shah (1933) and Bollywood actress Vidya Malvade (1973). Other notable events include the death of Assamese literature pioneer Chandra Kumar Agarwala (1938), the inauguration of India's first government-owned company, the Sindri Factory, by Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru (1952), and the births of Bollywood stars Tiger Shroff (1990).
