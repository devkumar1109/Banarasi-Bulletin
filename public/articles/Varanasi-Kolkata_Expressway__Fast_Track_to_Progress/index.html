Varanasi-Kolkata Expressway: Fast Track to Progress

2025-04-05T08:18:00+00:00

The Varanasi to Kolkata expressway, part of the Bharatmala Project, spans 610-690 km across Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Jharkhand, and West Bengal. Construction on the first phase, a 27 km stretch from Varanasi to Chainpur, has begun after successful land acquisition. The expressway aims to reduce travel time from 12-14 hours to 6 hours by allowing vehicles to travel at 120 kmph. It will cover 18 districts, boosting tourism, agriculture, and industry. The project, costing 35,000 crore rupees, is divided into 13 phases and is expected to create thousands of jobs. The longest stretch is in West Bengal (323 km) while the shortest is in Uttar Pradesh (22 km).
