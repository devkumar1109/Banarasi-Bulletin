Delhi Rain Brings Relief from Heatwave

2025-06-14T07:19:00+00:00

Delhi experienced temperatures exceeding 42°C last week, but relief is expected with rain and thunderstorms predicted by the India Meteorological Department (IMD). The IMD forecasts light to moderate rainfall with thunderstorms and gusty winds (reaching 40-50 kmph) across Delhi, Haryana, Chandigarh, and other northwestern regions from June 13-19. Delhi's maximum temperature today is expected to be around 39-41°C, with the minimum around 28-30°C due to the anticipated rainfall. The orange alert issued due to heat waves has been lifted. A western disturbance is expected to impact northwest India, bringing the rain and thunderstorms. Other northwestern states will also experience weather changes, with heavy rainfall in Uttarakhand until June 19 and Uttar Pradesh between June 17 and 19. Rajasthan may see dust storms and thundersqualls. Delhi's air quality is currently 'moderate' according to the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB), with some areas recording 'poor' AQI.
