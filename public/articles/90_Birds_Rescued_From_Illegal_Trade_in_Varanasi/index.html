90 Birds Rescued From Illegal Trade in Varanasi

2025-05-03T18:22:00+00:00

In Varanasi, forest officials and police rescued 90 birds from illegal trade in Baheliya Tola. The rescued species included Indian Parrots, Red Avadavats, Buntings, Budgerigars, and Java Sparrows. The operation, conducted on April 28, led to the arrest of the culprits. The birds were taken to Sarnath Zoo for medical care and were subsequently released. Varanasi is vulnerable to wildlife trafficking due to its geographic location. Baheliya Tola, near the city station, is a known hub for illegal wildlife trade linked to Bihar, Jharkhand, and West Bengal. A forest crime case has been registered, and the apprehended individuals are in jail.
