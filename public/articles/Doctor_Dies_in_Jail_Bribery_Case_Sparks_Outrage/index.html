Doctor Dies in Jail: Bribery Case Sparks Outrage

2025-06-17T14:05:00+00:00

Dr. Venkatesh Maur, superintendent of Bansdih Community Health Centre (CHC) in Ballia, died in Varanasi district jail on Monday. He was arrested last Thursday for allegedly accepting a bribe of Rs 20,000 from a PMBJAK operator. Doctors in Ballia are protesting his death, demanding a CBI inquiry and alleging foul play. The Provincial Medical Services Association (PMSA) claims Dr. Maur was healthy before arrest and suspects he was falsely implicated. They have suspended OPD services in all government health centers as a mark of protest. Retired IPS officer Amitabh Thakur also demanded an inquiry, alleging inhuman treatment and extortion by jail officials.
