Monkey Mischief Damages Varanasi Temple Spire

2025-03-19T16:26:00+00:00

Monkeys damaged a 19th-century golden 'kalash' (spire) atop the Baikuntheshwar Mahadev section of the Kashi Vishwanath temple in Varanasi during Mangla Aarti. The 'kalash', plated with gold donated by Maharaja Ranjit Singh, fell but thankfully caused no injuries. Temple officials secured the damaged 'kalash' and enlisted artisans for repair. Measures are being taken to address the monkey menace and prevent future incidents. The temple's gold plating was initially done with gold donated by Maharaja Ranjit Singh in the 18th century, then further enhanced after a recent renovation project in 2021.
