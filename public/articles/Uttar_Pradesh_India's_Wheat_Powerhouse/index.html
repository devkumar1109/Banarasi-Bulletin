Uttar Pradesh: India's Wheat Powerhouse

2025-04-27T06:30:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh is India's highest wheat producer, accounting for 35.34 million tonnes in 2024. Its fertile plains, efficient irrigation systems (utilizing rivers like the Ganga and Yamuna), and favorable climate (Rabi season) contribute to this. Over 35% of the state's cropped area is dedicated to wheat. Madhya Pradesh ranks second with 22.58 million tonnes, followed by Punjab (17.74 million tonnes), and Haryana (12.08 million tonnes). These states benefit from high-yield farming, modern techniques, and strong infrastructure.
