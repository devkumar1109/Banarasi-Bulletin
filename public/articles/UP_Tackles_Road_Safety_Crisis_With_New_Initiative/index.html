U.P. Tackles Road Safety Crisis With New Initiative

2025-03-08T00:30:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath has launched a comprehensive road safety initiative to address the alarming rise in road accidents. In 2024, the state recorded 46,052 accidents, resulting in over 24,000 deaths and 34,600 injuries. Overspeeding, drunken driving, wrong-way movement, signal jumping, and mobile phone use while driving were identified as key contributing factors. The CM's directives include making highways access-controlled and establishing trauma centres, hospitals, and emergency facilities along expressways. Monthly district-level road safety committee meetings and quarterly reviews at the divisional level have also been ordered to ensure effective implementation of these measures.
