Varanasi's Smoky Brinjal Gets GI Tag

2025-05-27T12:50:00+00:00

Ramnagar Bhanta, a brinjal variety from Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, received a GI tag in 2023. Cultivated in Varanasi, Mirzapur, Chandauli, and Sonbhadra districts, it is prized for its large size (2-2.5 kg), rich, slightly smoky flavor, and nutritional value. Its smooth, dense flesh cooks into a creamy texture, making it ideal for dishes like bati-chokha. The brinjal changes from greenish-white to golden yellow as it ripens, signaling its readiness for seed extraction, a traditional farming practice. Ramnagar Bhanta supports local cuisine, traditional farming, and farmer livelihoods through sustainable cultivation and seed preservation.
