Banker Arrested in Rs 1.2 Crore Cyber Fraud

2025-04-24T18:33:00+00:00

Deepak Kumar, a relationship manager at a private bank in Uttar Pradesh, was arrested by Hyderabad Cybercrime Police for his role in a Rs 1.2 crore trading fraud case. Kumar allegedly provided a bank account to cyber fraudsters through Telegram for a commission. This account was linked to 23 cybercrime cases across India, with Rs 6 crore in transactions. A Hyderabad resident lost Rs 1.2 crore due to this fraud, prompting the police investigation.
