Chhattisgarh: Silicon Valley Meets GaN

2025-04-11T18:03:00+00:00

Chhattisgarh Chief Minister Vishnu Deo Sai laid the foundation stone for India's first Gallium Nitride (GaN)-based semiconductor fabrication unit in Nava Raipur. The Rs 1,143 crore plant, developed by Polymatech Electronics Limited, will commence commercial production by April-May 2026. It will manufacture high-frequency GaN chips for 5G and 6G telecommunications, with an aim to produce 10 billion chips annually by 2030. The facility is expected to generate thousands of direct jobs and lakhs of indirect employment opportunities, attract FDI, and boost regional economic development. Polymatech will also handle advanced packaging of the chips at the Raipur facility, which will serve as a hub for innovation in RF systems, defense electronics, AI, data analytics, and high-performance computing.
