Navratri Feast Ban: Meat, Eggs, and Fish Off the Menu

2025-03-29T11:21:00+00:00

The Maihar district administration in Madhya Pradesh has banned the sale of meat, fish, and eggs from March 30 to April 7 during Chaitra Navratri. The ban, issued under Section 163 of the Indian Civil Defence Code, 2023, aims to respect the religious sentiments of devotees visiting the renowned Maa Sharda Temple. Violators face legal action under Section 223 of the Indian Penal Code. Similarly, the Varanasi Municipal Corporation has closed all meat, fish, and poultry shops during Chaitra Navratri, coinciding with Eid celebrations. This decision, taken to honor religious sentiments, also extends to banning cigarette sales near religious sites and schools.
