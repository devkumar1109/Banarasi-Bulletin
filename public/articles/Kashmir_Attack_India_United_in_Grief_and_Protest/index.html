Kashmir Attack: India United in Grief and Protest

2025-04-25T18:02:00+00:00

Following a terror attack in Pahalgam, Jammu and Kashmir, that killed 26 people, protests erupted across Uttar Pradesh. Hindus, Muslims, traders, and political leaders united in condemnation. Rallies, candlelight marches, and signature campaigns were held, with Muslim communities in Muzaffarnagar wearing black badges and observing a shutdown. Traders in Kannauj, Bareilly, and Ghaziabad shut down businesses, and effigies of Pakistan were burned in various locations. Islamic scholar Allama Tauqeer Raza Khan urged calm and unity against terrorism, praising local Kashmiri Muslims who aided victims. BJP MP Atul Garg highlighted the Centre's diplomatic actions, including suspending the Indus Waters Treaty.
