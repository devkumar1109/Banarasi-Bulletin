Birds Freed: Varanasi Busts Wildlife Ring

2025-05-03T18:22:00+00:00

Forest officials and police in Varanasi rescued 90 birds from an illegal trade operation in Baheliya Tola. The rescued birds included Indian Parrots, Red Avadavats, Buntings, Budgerigars, and Java Sparrows. The raid on April 28th led to the arrest of the culprits and the birds were taken to Sarnath Zoo for medical care. The birds were released back into the wild on May 1st. Varanasi is considered a sensitive area for wildlife trafficking due to its location near forests and the Ganga river. Baheliya Tola has a history of illegal wildlife trade connected to Bihar, Jharkhand, and West Bengal. A forest crime case has been registered, and the apprehended individuals have been jailed.
