Adityanath: Frequent Elections Hinder India's Rise

2025-04-08T00:45:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath stated that frequent elections destabilize the political landscape, hinder national development, and promote corruption. He emphasized that political instability prevents India from achieving its goals of sovereignty, strength, and development. Adityanath argued that frequent elections negatively impact GDP, disrupt development schemes, and erode public trust in democracy. He highlighted the government's commitment to implementing simultaneous elections by 2034, based on recommendations from a committee headed by former President Ram Nath Kovind. He contrasted the current situation with the state's condition before 2017, alleging the existence of parallel administrations and lawlessness that hampered development. Adityanath urged citizens to support the 'One Nation, One Election' initiative and counter misinformation.
