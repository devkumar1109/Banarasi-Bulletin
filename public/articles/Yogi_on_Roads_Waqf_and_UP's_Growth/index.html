Yogi on Roads, Waqf, and UP's Growth

2025-04-01T09:10:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath defended his administration's ban on street prayers, stating roads are for traffic. He urged Muslims to emulate Hindu discipline citing the Kumbh Mela as an example. Adityanath criticized the Waqf Bill's critics, alleging Waqf boards are corrupt and focus on property grabbing instead of Muslim welfare. He believes the reform will ultimately benefit Muslims. He reiterated his commitment to strong law enforcement and the use of bulldozers to remove illegal encroachments, dismissing concerns about legal implications. Adityanath downplayed speculation about his future as Prime Minister, emphasizing his role as a Yogi. He denied any rift with the BJP leadership and highlighted the RSS's support. He asserted Uttar Pradesh is on track to become India's largest economy by 2030, claiming its per capita income has doubled under his leadership. He criticized the Samajwadi Party for its alleged ties to butchers and encouragement of cow slaughter during its tenure.
