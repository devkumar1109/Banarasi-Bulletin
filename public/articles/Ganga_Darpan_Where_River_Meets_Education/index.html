Ganga Darpan: Where River Meets Education

2025-05-20T21:30:00+00:00

The Ganga Darpan Interpretation Centre in Varanasi, developed by Wildlife Institute of India under the Namami Gange project, promotes biodiversity education and river conservation. The centre features a 3D model of the Ganga River showcasing its diverse wildlife including dolphins, crocodiles, and turtles. It attracts over 20,000 visitors annually with workshops, educational sessions, and programs. The centre highlights the Ganga's historical and cultural significance, from ancient times to Mughal rule, and showcases over 62 bird species, making it a haven for birdwatchers. Interactive kiosks and QR codes provide detailed information about the river's heritage and biodiversity.
