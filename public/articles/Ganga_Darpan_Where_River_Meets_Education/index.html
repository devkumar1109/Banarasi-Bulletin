Ganga Darpan: Where River Meets Education

2025-05-20T21:30:00+00:00

The Ganga Darpan interpretation centre in Varanasi promotes biodiversity education and river conservation. Developed under the Namami Gange project, the centre features a 3D model of the Ganga River showcasing its diverse wildlife, including dolphins, crocodiles, and turtles. The centre attracts over 20,000 visitors annually, offering workshops and educational sessions. It highlights the river's significance in people's lives and aims to shift perceptions from polluted to appreciated for its natural beauty. The 3D model displays the river's journey, including major pilgrimage sites, cities, and protected areas. A panel showcases the Ganga's historical and cultural significance across empires, referencing Ain-i-Akbari and Baburnama. Interactive kiosks provide detailed information about the river's heritage and biodiversity.
