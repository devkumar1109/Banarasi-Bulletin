Hindi Row: Adityanath vs. Stalin in Language Clash

2025-03-27T08:00:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath criticized DMK leader MK Stalin for opposing the three-language policy, accusing him of divisive politics for fearing a threat to his vote bank. Adityanath stressed the importance of learning Hindi and questioned DMK's opposition to it. Stalin, in response, called Adityanath's remarks "political black comedy," stating that Tamil Nadu opposes the “imposition and chauvinism” of languages, not languages themselves. He emphasized that the DMK stands against any language-based divisions.
