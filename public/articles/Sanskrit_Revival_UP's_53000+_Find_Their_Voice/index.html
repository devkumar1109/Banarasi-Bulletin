Sanskrit Revival: UP's 53,000+ Find Their Voice

2025-05-29T12:35:00+00:00

Over 53,000 people, half of them women, have been trained in spoken Sanskrit in Uttar Pradesh under an initiative led by Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath. Launched in 2018, the program aims to revive the ancient language through innovative methods like the 'Missed Call Yojana'. Participants underwent a 20-day structured training program. Additional initiatives like special sessions and internships were designed to deepen understanding and practice. The 'Grihe-Grihe Sanskritam' initiative brings the language into homes and communities, showing promising results.
