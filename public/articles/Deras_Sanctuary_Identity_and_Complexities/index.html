Deras: Sanctuary, Identity, and Complexities

2025-04-06T04:49:00+00:00

Santosh K Singh's book "The Deras: Culture, Diversity and Politics" examines the syncretic nature of deras, sacred spaces in Punjab, often seen through the lens of Dera Sacha Sauda. Singh argues that deras, run by gurus, have historically served as spaces for marginalized communities, particularly Valmikis and Chamars, offering shelter and countering caste hegemony. The deras often celebrate the legends of Ambedkar and Ravidas, offering a distinct identity for Dalits. However, the book also acknowledges the influence of class dynamics within these spaces. For example, Dera Ballan, while embracing Ravidassia identity and respecting Sikh traditions, also demonstrates the complexities of social and religious interactions within deras.
