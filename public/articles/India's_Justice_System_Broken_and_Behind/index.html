India's Justice System: Broken and Behind

2025-04-28T11:54:00+00:00

The India Justice Report 2025 reveals significant flaws in India's justice system. Key findings include: * **Underfunding and Staffing Shortages:** Police training receives only 1.25% of budgets, forensic science is understaffed, and prisons are overcrowded. * **Gender Inequality:** No state meets its reserved quotas for women in police forces. * **Overburdened Judiciary:** Over 5 crore cases are pending across courts, with only 4% initiated suo motu. * **Declining Legal Aid:** Paralegal volunteers have decreased since 2019, limiting access to legal advice, especially in rural areas. * **Slow Pace of Reform:** Despite some progress in areas like digital infrastructure and women in the judiciary, the overall picture is one of systemic neglect. The report calls for urgent and systemic reforms to ensure justice for all.
