Fake Cardiologist Arrested, Seven Deaths Under Scrutiny

2025-04-09T01:30:00+00:00

Narendra Vikramaditya Yadav, accused of posing as UK-based cardiologist Dr. John Camm, has been arrested in Madhya Pradesh. He allegedly used the stolen identity to secure a job at Damoh’s Mission Hospital, where at least seven patient deaths are being investigated. Dr. Camm confirmed the identity theft, stating it began five years ago with a Twitter account impersonating him. The account even urged India to send UP CM Yogi Adityanath to France during riots, a tweet that was retweeted by the CM himself. Indian cardiologists raised concerns about the authenticity of Yadav's claims and credentials, while police are verifying his alleged medical procedures. Yadav was sent to five days police custody.
