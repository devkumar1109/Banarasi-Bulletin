Uttar Pradesh Braces for Thunderstorms, Rain

2025-04-17T07:25:00+00:00

The India Meteorological Department (IMD) has issued an alert for thunderstorms, rain, and strong winds in 36 Uttar Pradesh districts between April 18 and 20. Eastern and central Uttar Pradesh are expected to be most affected, with wind speeds ranging from 30 to 50 km/hour. Districts like Banda, Chitrakoot, Prayagraj, Varanasi, Gorakhpur, Kanpur Nagar, and Saharanpur will experience these weather disruptions. The Terai belt will see light showers on April 17, with eastern Uttar Pradesh experiencing heavier rainfall and thunderstorms from April 18 onward. Night temperatures are predicted to be unusually warm and humid in several districts, including Aligarh, Mathura, and Agra. Temperatures across the state are already rising; Bulandshahr and Fatehpur recorded 40°C, while Lucknow reached 37.4°C. The IMD forecasts a gradual increase of 2-3 degrees Celsius in maximum temperatures over the next few days. Weather conditions are expected to stabilize by April 21.
