Hate Speech Costs: MLA Jailed for Election Rhetoric

2025-06-01T19:36:00+00:00

Abbas Ansari, an SBSP MLA from Mau Sadar, was sentenced to two years imprisonment for making hate speech during the 2022 Uttar Pradesh assembly elections. The CJM (MP-MLA) court in Mau found Ansari guilty of threatening state government officials and inciting communal tension. The court emphasized that hate speech is unacceptable in public service, especially during elections, and that it undermines democratic values. Ansari's speech, where he threatened officials with post-election retribution, was deemed a serious offense as it aimed to influence the electoral process and instilled fear among voters. The court considered Ansari's criminal history and his family's political legacy while imposing the sentence.
