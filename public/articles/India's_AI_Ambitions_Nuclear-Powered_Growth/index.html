India's AI Ambitions: Nuclear-Powered Growth

2025-02-24T12:18:00+00:00

India is strategically investing in AI growth by focusing on a comprehensive AI stack encompassing semiconductors, data centers, foundational models, and energy management powered by nuclear energy. External Affairs Minister Dr. S Jaishankar highlighted India's technological prowess and Prime Minister Modi's participation in the AI Action Summit in Paris, emphasizing the nation's commitment to AI leadership. India aims to establish domestic semiconductor fabrication facilities and develop low-cost, sovereign large language models (LLMs) tailored to Indian context and culture. The 2025-2026 Union Budget allocated Rs 20,000 crore for research and development of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs), aiming to increase India's nuclear capacity to 100 GW by 2047. This investment is crucial for powering AI's computational demands, ensuring sustainable energy for AI infrastructure, and maintaining India's competitiveness in the AI race.
