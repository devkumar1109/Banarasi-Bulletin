HCL-Foxconn Chip Plant to Power India's Electronics

2025-05-14T19:14:00+00:00

HCL and Foxconn's Rs 3,706 crore joint venture, approved by the Cabinet, will establish a semiconductor assembly and test unit in Jewar, Uttar Pradesh. The facility will manufacture display driver chips for smartphones and other electronics, aiming to meet 40% of India's demand. Production is expected to begin in 2027, with a capacity of 36 million chips per month. The Centre will provide Rs 1,800 crore in subsidies, while the UP government will contribute Rs 600 crore. This marks the sixth semiconductor unit under the India Semiconductor Mission and the first in Uttar Pradesh.
