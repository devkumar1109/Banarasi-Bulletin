Cat Burning: Friends Face Wildlife Charges

2025-03-10T13:10:00+00:00

A woman named Priya and her friends have been booked under the Wildlife Protection Act for burning a wild cat alive in Moradabad, Uttar Pradesh. The incident occurred after the cat crossed their path, and the group allegedly filmed the act. Delhi's Wildlife Crime Control Bureau received an email complaint with the video, prompting police action. The video showed the group burning the cat while riding a bike. Police traced the accused using the bike's registration number. The accused face up to three years in prison and a fine of Rs 1 lakh if found guilty.
