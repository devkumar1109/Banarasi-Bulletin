UP Aims for Trillion-Dollar Economy by 2029

2025-04-25T06:57:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh CM Yogi Adityanath aims to transform the state into a one-trillion-dollar economy by 2029. Despite claims of UP being the second-largest economy, its projected GDP in 2024-25 places it fifth, behind Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Gujarat. The state government has received ₹45 lakh crore in investment proposals since 2018, with ₹15 lakh crore already implemented, creating 60 lakh jobs. UP's unemployment rate at 2.4% is below the national average of 3.2%. To achieve the trillion-dollar goal, the government is planning another Investors' Summit with a target of ₹33 lakh crore in investments, aiming for ₹100 lakh crore by 2027. Invest UP offices will be set up in Mumbai, Delhi, and Bangalore. Digital land use changes and a GIS data bank for industrial land are also being implemented. The government has established the Bundelkhand Industrial Development Authority to address regional imbalances, allocating ₹5,000 crore for development on 56,000 acres.
