UP Aims for Trillion-Dollar Economy by 2029

2025-04-25T06:57:00+00:00

Despite claims of being India's second-largest economy, Uttar Pradesh currently holds the fifth position with a projected GDP of ₹45 lakh crore for 2024-25. Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath aims to transform the state into a trillion-dollar economy by 2029. Since 2018, the state has attracted ₹45 lakh crore in investment proposals, with ₹15 lakh crore already implemented, creating 60 lakh jobs. The state government is organizing an Investors' Summit with roadshows to attract ₹33 lakh crore in investments, aiming for a total of ₹100 lakh crore by 2027. To facilitate this growth, Invest UP offices will be established in Mumbai, Delhi, and Bangalore. Digitization of land use changes and a GIS data bank for industrial use are also being implemented. The Bundelkhand Industrial Development Authority has been set up to address regional imbalances, with 56,000 acres earmarked for development and ₹5,000 crore allocated.
