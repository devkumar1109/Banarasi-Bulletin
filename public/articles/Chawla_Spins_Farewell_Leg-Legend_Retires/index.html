Chawla Spins Farewell: Leg-Legend Retires

2025-06-06T10:24:00+00:00

Piyush Chawla, a two-time World Cup winner and leg-spinner, has announced his retirement from all formats of cricket. Chawla made his debut for India at 15, representing both the Under-19 and Uttar Pradesh U-22 teams. He played a crucial role in Uttar Pradesh's first Ranji Trophy win, taking 35 wickets and scoring 224 runs in his first first-class season. His international career included taking 14 wickets on India's 2007 tour of Ireland and England and 3 wickets in his ODI debut against Bangladesh in 2007. Despite a strong performance in the IPL, Chawla struggled to maintain a consistent spot in the Indian test squad after Anil Kumble's retirement.
