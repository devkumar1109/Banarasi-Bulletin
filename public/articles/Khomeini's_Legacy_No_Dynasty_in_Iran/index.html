Khomeini's Legacy: No Dynasty in Iran

2025-06-16T11:02:00+00:00

Ayatollah Ruhollah Khomeini, Iran's first Supreme Leader, led the 1979 Islamic Revolution. His grandfather, Ahmad Hindi, was born near Barabanki in Uttar Pradesh, India. Khomeini's opposition to hereditary rule is documented in "Sahifeyeh Imam Khomeini," a collection of his writings. He believed family-based rule was un-Islamic, a view shared by the current Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Despite speculation, Khamenei's son is unlikely to succeed him due to this principle. Tensions between Iran and Israel are high after an Israeli strike killed an Iranian commander. US President Donald Trump reportedly blocked an Israeli plan to assassinate Khamenei.
