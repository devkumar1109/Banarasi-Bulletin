Unpaid Threads: Weaving Inequality in Varanasi

2025-05-23T19:33:00+00:00

This article from the Economic and Political Weekly explores unpaid labor among women weavers in Varanasi, India. The study, based on 364 weaver households, highlights the prevalence and persistence of unpaid labor in the weaving industry across two generations of women within the same households. The authors argue that women's financial independence is crucial for their empowerment, emphasizing the need for gender-sensitive policies that recognize and compensate women's labor. The research draws upon Marxist feminist theories and acknowledges the historical marginalization of women's unpaid work in economic surveys and national income accounts.
