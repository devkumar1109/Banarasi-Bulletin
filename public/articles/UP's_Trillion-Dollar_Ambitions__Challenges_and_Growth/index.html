UP's Trillion-Dollar Ambitions: Challenges and Growth

2025-04-25T06:57:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath aims to transform the state into a trillion-dollar economy by 2029. Despite claims of being the second-largest economy, UP's projected GDP for 2024-25 places it fifth, behind Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Gujarat. The state's unemployment rate at 2.4% is below the national average of 3.2%. Since 2018, UP has received ₹45 lakh crore in investment proposals with ₹15 lakh crore already implemented, creating 60 lakh jobs. To achieve the trillion-dollar goal, the government plans another Investors' Summit targeting ₹33 lakh crore in investments, aiming for ₹100 lakh crore by 2027. Invest UP offices will be set up in Mumbai, Delhi, and Bangalore. Efforts are underway to digitize land use changes, create a GIS land bank, and tackle regional imbalances through the Bundelkhand Industrial Development Authority, which has been allocated ₹5,000 crore.
