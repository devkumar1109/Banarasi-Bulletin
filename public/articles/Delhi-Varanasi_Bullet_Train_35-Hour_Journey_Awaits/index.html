Delhi-Varanasi Bullet Train: 3.5-Hour Journey Awaits

2025-04-14T04:30:00+00:00

India is building a high-speed bullet train between Delhi and Varanasi, aiming to complete the 840-kilometer journey in 3.5 hours. The train will depart from Hazrat Nizamuddin station in Delhi and arrive at Manduadih station in Varanasi, stopping at 12 stations including Noida, Mathura, Agra, Lucknow, and Prayagraj. Estimated to cost Rs 43,000 crore, the project is expected to be finished by 2029. The Delhi-Varanasi High-Speed Rail Corridor will have 18 daily bullet trains running between 6 am and midnight, offering a train every 47 minutes. New infrastructure, including an underground station at Sarai Kale Khan in Delhi and a 15-kilometer tunnel, is being built to support the project.
