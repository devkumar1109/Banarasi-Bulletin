Yemen War Plans Leaked to Journalist

2025-03-25T07:30:00+00:00

US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth and top Trump officials mistakenly sent classified war plans for a Yemen strike to a journalist at The Atlantic. The leak, revealed just before the attack, has sparked outrage and calls for investigations. President Trump initially denied knowledge of the breach but later joked about it online. Officials were using the public messaging app Signal to discuss sensitive materials related to the Houthis, raising concerns about the use of unsecured platforms for military operations. Trump dismissed The Atlantic as a failing magazine and claimed no war plans were texted, downplaying the severity of the leak. The incident highlights issues with using public apps for sensitive discussions and raises questions about oversight and security protocols within the administration.
