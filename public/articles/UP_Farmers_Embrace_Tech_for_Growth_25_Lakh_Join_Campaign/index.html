UP Farmers Embrace Tech for Growth, 2.5 Lakh Join Campaign

2025-05-31T18:46:00+00:00

The Vikasit Krishi Sankalp Abhiyan 2025, launched by Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath, has seen 2.5 lakh farmers participate within three days. The scheme aims to boost agricultural productivity and farmer income by connecting them with new research technologies and government schemes. The UP government aims to reach 50 lakh farmers through 10,125 programs across 75 districts, a fraction of the national target of connecting 1.5 crore farmers. Farmer-scientist dialogues are being held at 135 locations per district until June 12. Union ministers Jitin Prasada, Anupriya Patel, and state agriculture minister Baldev Singh Aulakh participated in various programs, discussing topics like horticulture, plant protection, direct sowing of paddy, and crop insurance.
