India's Waqf Wealth: Hyderabad Leads the Way

2025-04-06T03:12:00+00:00

Hyderabad is known as the "Waqf capital" of India, housing around 77,000 Waqf properties, which is 30% of the country's total. Telangana's Waqf Board is the wealthiest, earning nearly Rs 500 crore annually from rent and donations. Uttar Pradesh has the highest number of Waqf properties at 1.5 lakh, but ranks second in total value. Notable places include Bada Imambara in Lucknow. Andhra Pradesh holds large farming lands in Kadapa and Anantapur, alongside sites like the Shahi Masjid in Kurnool. Telangana and Andhra Pradesh together hold about 1.2 lakh Waqf properties.
