Meghalaya's First Planned Murder: SIT Seeks Justice

2025-06-18T14:14:00+00:00

Meghalaya Minister Alexander Laloo Hek stated that the Raja Raghuvanshi murder case is the first instance of an organized, pre-planned crime in the state. Hek emphasized the government's commitment to swift justice and making Meghalaya crime-free. The SIT meticulously reconstructed the crime scene, including revisiting the river site and involving the key accused, Sonam, to gather evidence. Initially, both Sonam and Raja Raghuvanshi's families blamed Meghalaya and its people, but later apologized. The Meghalaya Police are continuing their investigations and aim to file a chargesheet within the stipulated time. The SIT reconstructed the crime scene in Sohra, with the accused and three others present. This helped the police understand the sequence of events leading to the murder. One machete used in the crime is still missing.
