Scorching India: Heatwave Grips Nation, Exacerbating Poverty

2025-03-16T10:44:00+00:00

India experienced unprecedented heat from March to June 2024, with 37 cities seeing temperatures exceeding 45 degrees Celsius. May 2024 was particularly severe, with temperatures averaging 31.7C, a degree higher than May 2023. Electricity demand surged 6.1% from April to September 2024 compared to the previous year, with air conditioning accounting for an estimated 30% of the increase. The heat disproportionately impacts the poor. Some, like Subhash Tiwari, were able to borrow money for air conditioners, while others, like Dimple Mishra, couldn't even afford a cooler. Rising electricity costs are forcing families into difficult situations, with some considering sending children to work. State-run healthcare facilities are overwhelmed, struggling to cope with the influx of heat-related illnesses.
