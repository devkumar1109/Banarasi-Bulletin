Supreme Court Denies Bail in Child Trafficking Cases

2025-04-15T15:15:00+00:00

The Supreme Court canceled the bail of 13 accused in child trafficking cases, criticizing the Allahabad High Court for its "callous" handling of the applications. The court expressed serious concern over the rising prevalence of inter-state child trafficking in India, noting diverse forms and evolving tactics employed by traffickers. It directed the Uttar Pradesh government to ensure education for trafficked children and expedited the trial against the accused in three FIRs registered in Varanasi. The court expressed disappointment with the state government's lack of action and failure to challenge the high court's bail orders, stating it exhibited "no seriousness worth the name."
