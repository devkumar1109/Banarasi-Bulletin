Nagar Vadhus Dance at Varanasi's Holy Fires

2025-04-05T08:49:00+00:00

During the three-day Shringar Festival in Varanasi, sex workers known as 'Nagar Vadhus' danced around burning pyres at Manikarnika Ghat, a tradition dating back hundreds of years. The festival, held from the fifth to seventh day of Chaitra Navratra, culminates with tantric rituals and devotional songs. The 'Nagar Vadhus' offer prayers at the temple of Mahashmashan Nath, hoping to attain salvation and leave their profession behind. The tradition began when Raja Sawai Man Singh of Amber renovated the temple and invited sex workers to perform music after other artists declined.
