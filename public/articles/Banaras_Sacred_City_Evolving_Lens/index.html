Banaras: Sacred City, Evolving Lens

2025-03-22T21:17:00+00:00

An exhibition at DAG in Delhi showcases the visual representation of Varanasi (Banaras) from colonial times to the present. The exhibition explores the city's vibrant essence, highlighting its sacred ghats and the rituals surrounding death and cremation. Photography and videography restrictions at the open cremation spaces were once strictly enforced but have relaxed in recent years. Varanasi is considered a sacred city for Hindus, with its banks of the Ganga attracting pilgrims and seekers of spiritual enlightenment for millennia. The exhibition captures the city's unique blend of the divine and the mundane, showcasing the daily life and rituals that coexist with the cycle of life and death.
