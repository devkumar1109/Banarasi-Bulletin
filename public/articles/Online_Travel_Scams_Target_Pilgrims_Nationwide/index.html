Online Travel Scams Target Pilgrims Nationwide

2025-04-22T00:30:00+00:00

The Indian Cyber Crime Coordination Centre (I4C) issued a nationwide alert warning of a surge in online travel scams targeting pilgrims and tourists. These scams involve fake websites, deceptive social media pages, and fraudulent advertisements. Victims are lured by attractive offers for VIP darshan, discounted hotel stays, and low-cost tour packages, only to discover they have been conned upon reaching their destination. One case involved a resident of Melkanda losing Rs 64,000 to a fake tour company promising a pilgrimage to Prayagraj. In another case, 35-year-old Raghavendra Rao was arrested for allegedly conning over 100 individuals in a fake Kumbh Mela tour scam.
