Uttar Pradesh Cracks Down on Road Safety Crisis

2025-03-08T00:30:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath has launched a comprehensive road safety initiative to address the alarming rise in accidents. In 2024, the state recorded 46,052 accidents, resulting in over 24,000 deaths and 34,600 injuries. Overspeeding, drunk driving, wrong-way movement, signal jumping, and mobile phone use while driving are the key contributing factors. The CM has directed officials to make highways access-controlled and establish trauma centers, hospitals, and emergency facilities along expressways. Monthly district-level road safety committee meetings and quarterly reviews at the divisional level have been mandated for effective implementation of these measures.
