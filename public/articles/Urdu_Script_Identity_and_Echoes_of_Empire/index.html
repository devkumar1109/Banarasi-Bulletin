Urdu: Script, Identity, and Echoes of Empire

2025-03-18T09:00:00+00:00

The recent controversy over Urdu in Uttar Pradesh highlights a long-standing historical conflict rooted in the colonial era. During British rule, Urdu was classified as a "Muslim language," contributing to the communalization of linguistic identities. This led to debates and policies that solidified language as an identity marker, with the script (Nagari vs. Persian) becoming a focal point. The current controversy echoes this past, with Urdu being vilified and associated solely with Muslim identity, despite its deep historical roots in North Indian literature and culture. Urdu evolved from the local Khariboli dialect, enriched by interactions with Persian-speaking communities.
