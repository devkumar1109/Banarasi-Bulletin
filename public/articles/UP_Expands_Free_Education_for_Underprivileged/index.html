UP Expands Free Education for Underprivileged

2025-03-24T13:26:00+00:00

The Uttar Pradesh government is expanding Jai Prakash Narayan Sarvodaya Vidyalayas to provide quality education to underprivileged children. The number of these schools will increase to 120 by 2024-25, up from 93 in 2017. Currently, 100 schools are operational, with 70 for boys and 30 for girls. Each school accommodates 490 students. The government is focusing on improving teaching standards by appointing qualified educators and organizing orientation programs. 43 schools are affiliated with the CBSE board, while 57 follow the UP Board curriculum. Schools have smart classrooms, computer labs, and digital resources. Admission is free and follows a reservation system, with priority given to economically weaker sections. Students receive free residential facilities, uniforms, textbooks, stationery, and nutritious meals. The government has allocated Rs 363.91 crore to support this initiative.
