Varanasi Transformed: Rs 40,000 Crore Development Boom

2025-03-27T19:00:00+00:00

Ravindra Jaiswal, Minister of state for stamp and court registration fees (Independent Charge), highlighted extensive development work in Varanasi over the past eight years. He stated that 458 projects worth over Rs 40,536 crores were completed, encompassing roads, bridges, drinking water, sewage, education, health, energy, tourism, urban development, and police welfare. Additionally, construction projects worth over Rs 15,000 crore are underway. Jaiswal emphasized the completion of 102 road and bridge projects worth over Rs 22,408 crore and the progress of projects worth over Rs 4,955 crore. Railway, airport, and waterway projects costing over Rs 1,529 crore were finished, with additional projects worth over Rs 3,809 crore under construction. The Smart City project saw the completion of 34 projects worth Rs 877.65 crore, including the Kashi Integrated Command Control Centre and the Varanasi Sports Complex. 60 sewerage and drinking water projects worth over Rs 1,000 crore were completed, with further projects worth over Rs 2,100 crore ongoing.
