Stalin vs. Adityanath: Three-Language Row Ignites

2025-03-27T11:58:00+00:00

Tamil Nadu Chief Minister MK Stalin criticized Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath for calling the opposition to the three-language policy "narrow politics." Stalin accused Adityanath of lecturing on "hate" while his party is rattled by nationwide opposition to the policy. Stalin clarified that the opposition isn't against any language but against imposition and chauvinism. He stressed that the issue is about dignity and justice, not vote bank politics. Adityanath, in response, accused Stalin of attempting to divide people based on region and language for political gain. He questioned the hatred towards Hindi, highlighting the historical significance of Tamil and its connection to Indian culture.
