Varanasi Embraces 3D: Smart City Future Unveiled

2025-04-25T17:47:00+00:00

Varanasi is the first city in India to use 3D mapping for urban planning and development. The project, costing Rs 6 crore and completed in nine months, utilizes 3D Urban Spatial Digital Twin technology incorporating LiDAR, 360-degree imagery, and 3D modelling. The 3D model covers 160 sq km, showcasing the city's alleys, Ganga river, temples, and key locations. This technology will aid in sustainable development, showcasing Kashi's heritage and providing precise measurements for planning. Key benefits include real-time flood level monitoring, efficient crowd management, crime control, traffic assessment, and improved waste, sewage, and water management.
