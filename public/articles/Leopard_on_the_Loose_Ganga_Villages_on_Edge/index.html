Leopard on the Loose, Ganga Villages on Edge

2025-05-27T20:59:00+00:00

Following a leopard attack in Varanasi last week that injured three people, 12 villages along the Ganga river are on high alert. The leopard, suspected to have come from Chandauli or the Kaimur Wildlife Sanctuary, remains at large despite a massive search operation by forest teams. The injured are in stable condition. Villagers are fearful and taking precautions, with children and the elderly advised to limit their outings. Nightly vigils with sticks and firecrackers are being held to deter the animal. The forest department has deployed guards, set up nets and cages, and conducted combing operations. The leopard's exact whereabouts are unknown.
