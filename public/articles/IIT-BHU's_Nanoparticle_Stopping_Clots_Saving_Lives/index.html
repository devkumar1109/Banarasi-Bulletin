IIT-BHU's Nanoparticle: Stopping Clots, Saving Lives

2025-05-06T17:55:00+00:00

A team at IIT-BHU developed Potassium Ferric Oxalate Nanoparticle (KFeOx-NPs) to prevent blood clotting and thrombosis. This novel material effectively stops clot formation for up to 48 hours without altering blood cell structure. It offers a safer alternative to current anticoagulants like warfarin and heparin, which can cause side effects like bleeding and bone density loss. KFeOx-NPs also addresses limitations of chemical anticoagulants used in blood collection tubes by preventing structural changes in blood cells. The research, published in ACS Applied Materials & Interfaces, demonstrated the material's efficacy in both human blood samples and a mouse model. A patent has been filed to protect the intellectual property and facilitate clinical translation.
