UP's GCC Policy: Tech Hubs, Jobs, and Big Incentives

2025-05-19T04:32:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh's Global Capability Centres (GCC) Policy 2024 aims to transform cities like Noida, Lucknow, Kanpur, and Varanasi into technology and digital service hubs. The policy targets attracting Fortune 500 companies and boosting foreign direct investment, with a goal of creating over 2 lakh high-paying jobs in the next five years. Incentives include land cost subsidies (30-50%), stamp duty exemption, capital subsidies, SGST reimbursement, interest subsidies, operational subsidies, and payroll support. Companies setting up Level-1 GCCs need a minimum investment of Rs 15 crore (outside Gautam Buddha Nagar and Ghaziabad) or Rs 20 crore within those districts, with a minimum of 500 employees. Advanced GCCs require Rs 50 crore (outside) or Rs 75 crore (within) with 1,000 employees.
