Yogi Targets Food, Drug Fakes as Social Crime

2025-05-14T07:59:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath has declared food adulteration and sale of fake medicines as a "social crime" and vowed strict action against offenders. He directed the display of their photos at public places to deter others and raise awareness. Adityanath ordered daily consumables like oil, ghee, spices, milk, and cheese to be regularly tested. Special teams will focus on milk and dairy product checks. The CM emphasized public health as the state's priority and highlighted the expansion of food and drug laboratories. He suggested a 'Corpus Fund' for laboratory maintenance and stressed coordination with police to curb the fake drug trade. Transparency in the food safety process is ensured through a barcode system and a mobile app, 'Food Safety Connect'. The CM directed that complaints should be resolved to the complainant's satisfaction. Significant investment is being made in medical device and pharmaceutical manufacturing, creating jobs.
