Honeymoon Homicide: Sonam, Accomplices in Custody

2025-06-11T13:57:00+00:00

Sonam Raghuvanshi and her four accomplices have been granted eight-day police custody by a Shillong court. They are accused of murdering Sonam's husband, Raja Raghuvanshi, during their honeymoon in Meghalaya. Police aim to reconstruct the crime scene in Sohra with the accused's help. Sonam was arrested from Ghazipur, Uttar Pradesh, while her aides were apprehended from Indore, Madhya Pradesh. Raja's body was found in a gorge on June 2nd. Initially, authorities suspected abduction, but the case took a turn when Sonam confessed to the murder, revealing a pre-planned scheme fueled by an affair. Sonam's brother, Govind Raghuvanshi, has apologized to Raja's family, stating his belief in his sister's guilt based on available evidence.
