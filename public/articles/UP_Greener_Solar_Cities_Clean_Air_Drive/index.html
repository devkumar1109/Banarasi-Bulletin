UP Greener, Solar Cities, Clean Air Drive

2025-03-13T15:05:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath stated that 210 crore trees have been planted in the state over the last eight years, resulting in a significant increase in forest cover despite rapid development. The government is monitoring the survival rate of these trees through third-party assessments by the Forest Research Institute and a Chhattisgarh university. Adityanath announced plans to transform 17 municipal corporation areas into "solar cities," following the model of Ayodhya, the state's first solar city. He expressed concern over rising pollution in the Delhi-NCR region and highlighted the impact of burning wood and coal on air quality and health. He urged people to adopt cleaner fuels.
