UP Schools Scorch, Holidays Extended to June 30th

2025-06-14T17:14:00+00:00

The Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Council extended school holidays for classes up to 8th grade until June 30th due to a severe heatwave. The original reopening date was June 16th. While schools are closed, teachers and staff are required to be present. Academic activities will resume on July 1st. UP Deputy Chief Minister Brajesh Pathak urged citizens to exercise caution during the heatwave, noting that hospitals are on high alert for heat-related emergencies. The heatwave has particularly impacted South Uttar Pradesh, with gradual respite expected from June 14th in Northwest India according to the India Meteorological Department. Ayodhya District Magistrate Nikhil Tikaram Funde highlighted the district's action plan to mitigate the heatwave's effects, including ensuring access to cold water for residents and tourists, providing shade, and reserving hospital beds for heatstroke victims.
