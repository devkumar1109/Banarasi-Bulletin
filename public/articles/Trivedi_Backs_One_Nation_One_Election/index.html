Trivedi Backs "One Nation, One Election"

2025-02-27T18:45:00+00:00

BJP MP Sudhanshu Trivedi advocates for "One Nation, One Election," arguing it aligns with the Constitution's original intent and promotes economic development and political stability. He cites the frequent elections from 1989 to 2014 as a period of instability compared to the stable government formed under PM Modi in 2014. Trivedi believes simultaneous elections will allow for long-term development plans and reduce administrative burden. He also highlights the success of direct benefit transfer schemes, citing 54 crore Jan Dhan accounts opened and Rs 2 lakh crore deposited, benefiting the poor. Trivedi acknowledges opposition to the idea but emphasizes the government's determination to implement it, expecting improved politics, administrative efficiency, and economic benefits.
