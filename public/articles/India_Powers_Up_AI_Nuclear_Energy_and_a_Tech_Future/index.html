India Powers Up: AI, Nuclear Energy, and a Tech Future

2025-02-24T12:18:00+00:00

India is strategically investing in AI development, aiming to build a complete AI stack from semiconductor fabrication to energy management. This includes establishing domestic semiconductor manufacturing capabilities, developing low-cost large language models (LLMs) tailored to Indian context, and significantly increasing nuclear energy capacity. The Union Budget 2025-2026 allocated Rs 20,000 crore for research and development of Small Modular Reactors (SMRs) to achieve 100 GW nuclear energy capacity by 2047. Nuclear energy is seen as a reliable and cost-effective source to power AI's high energy demands. India's External Affairs Minister, S Jaishankar, emphasized the nation's historical role as a technology provider and highlighted Prime Minister Modi's participation in the AI Action Summit in Paris.
