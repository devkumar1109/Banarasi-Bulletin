Han Duck-soo Back: Korea's Political Earthquake

2025-03-24T07:30:00+00:00

South Korea's Constitutional Court reinstated Han Duck-soo as acting president, overturning his impeachment. This decision comes amidst political turmoil fueled by U.S. trade tariffs and internal unrest, including controversy over President Yoon Suk Yeol's use of martial law. The court's surprise ruling raises questions about South Korea's political future and potential for further instability. President Yoon's actions have been scrutinized, particularly his handling of the martial law situation. The reinstatement of Han Duck-soo is a significant political event with potential long-term consequences for leadership and policies. U.S. trade tariffs have added economic pressure on South Korea, impacting various sectors. The government's response to these challenges will be crucial in the coming months.
