India's Sugarcane Capitals: A Sweet Chart

2025-06-07T13:04:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh is India's largest sugarcane producer, contributing 2252.2 lakh tons in 2022-23. Its fertile river plains and well-established irrigation support high yields. Key sugarcane districts include Kanpur, Prayagraj, and Varanasi. Maharashtra ranks second with 1239.7 lakh tons, mainly produced in the Deccan Plateau region. Karnataka follows with 624.6 lakh tons, focusing on Hassan, Tumkur, and Chikmagalur districts. Tamil Nadu produced 169.2 lakh tons, with key districts including Dindigul, Nilgiris, and Erode. Sugarcane is a crucial cash crop in India, providing jobs to over ten lakh people and making India the second-largest sugarcane producer globally.
