Indian-Origin Mayor Makes History in England

2025-05-16T04:28:00+00:00

Raj Mishra, a 37-year-old from a village in Mirzapur, Uttar Pradesh, has been elected mayor of Wellingborough, a town in North Northamptonshire, England. Mishra, a Conservative Party councillor, is of Indian origin and represents a small Indian population in the town. He moved to London six years ago to pursue an MTech in advanced computer science, and later worked for the Ministry of Defence and in the banking sector. Mishra was elected to the town council in May and subsequently became the fifth mayor of Wellingborough. His victory has brought joy to his family in India, who celebrated his achievement.
