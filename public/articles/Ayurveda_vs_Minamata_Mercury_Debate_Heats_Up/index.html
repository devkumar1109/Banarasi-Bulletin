Ayurveda vs. Minamata: Mercury Debate Heats Up

2025-06-09T18:58:00+00:00

A professor from Banaras Hindu University, Anand K Chaudhary, has urged Prime Minister Narendra Modi to ensure the availability of mercury for Ayurvedic medicine manufacturing. Chaudhary argues that India, while a signatory to the Minamata Convention aiming to reduce mercury use, should preserve its traditional knowledge system by allowing controlled use of mercury for Ayurvedic purposes. He points out that Indian acharyas developed "Rasoshadhi," a medicinal form of mercury, centuries ago and its use in Ayurvedic formulations has been safe and effective for treating various diseases.
