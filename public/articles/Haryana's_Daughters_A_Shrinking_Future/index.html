Haryana's Daughters: A Shrinking Future

2025-06-02T05:14:00+00:00

Haryana is facing a declining sex ratio at birth, with 480 villages recording below 700 girls per 1000 boys. In 2024, the state's sex ratio dropped to 910, the lowest in eight years. Ghrouli village, for example, has seen 89 girls born against 128 boys in the past five years. Social pressure plays a role, with families feeling obligated to have a son for societal acceptance and future care of parents. Families with agricultural land often limit their family size if their first child is a boy, fearing land division.
