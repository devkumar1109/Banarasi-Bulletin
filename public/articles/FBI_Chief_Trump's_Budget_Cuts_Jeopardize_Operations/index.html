FBI Chief: Trump's Budget Cuts Jeopardize Operations

2025-05-08T06:30:00+00:00

FBI Director Kash Patel criticizes President Trump's proposed $545 million budget cut for the FBI, warning it will harm core operations. Patel argues the FBI needs $11.1 billion to function without layoffs, emphasizing the current proposal forces difficult decisions regarding staffing and resources. He is negotiating with Congress to reverse the cut, highlighting tensions between Trump's financial plans and law enforcement needs. The proposed budget prioritizes counterterrorism and fieldwork while reducing D.C. overhead and diversity programs. Patel explains that the "skinny budget" forces 904 positions to relocate from Washington, D.C., to field offices, aiming to improve crime management but raising concerns about potential impacts on intelligence capabilities.
