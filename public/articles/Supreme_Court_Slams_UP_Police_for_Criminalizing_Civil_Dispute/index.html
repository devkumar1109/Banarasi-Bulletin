Supreme Court Slams UP Police for Criminalizing Civil Dispute

2025-04-07T12:32:00+00:00

The Supreme Court criticized the Uttar Pradesh Police for filing criminal charges in a civil dispute involving a loan of Rs 25 lakh. The Court stated that this practice constitutes a "breakdown of the rule of law" and violates its previous direction in the case of Sharif Ahmed vs State of Uttar Pradesh. The bench, led by Chief Justice Sanjiv Khanna, ordered the Director General of Police and the Station House Officer to file affidavits demonstrating compliance with the Sharif Ahmed judgment. The Court stayed proceedings in the trial court against the accused while allowing proceedings under the Negotiable Instruments Act to continue.
