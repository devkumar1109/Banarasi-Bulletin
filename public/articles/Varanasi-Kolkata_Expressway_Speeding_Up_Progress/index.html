Varanasi-Kolkata Expressway: Speeding Up Progress

2025-04-05T08:18:00+00:00

Construction has begun on the Varanasi-Kolkata expressway, a 610-690 km project under the Bharatmala Project. The expressway will span Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, Jharkhand, and West Bengal, aiming to reduce travel time from 12-14 hours to 6 hours. The speed limit will be 120 kmph. The first phase, a 27 km stretch from Varanasi to Chainpur in Kaimur, is underway with land acquisition already completed. The expressway is expected to boost tourism, agriculture, and industry in the 18 districts it will pass through. It will create thousands of jobs and provide direct connectivity for hundreds of thousands of people. The project cost is 35,000 crore rupees.
