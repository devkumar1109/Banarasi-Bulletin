India's Crumbling Bridges: Another Tragedy Strikes

2025-06-15T07:44:00+00:00

On Sunday, a bridge over the Indrayani river in Pune collapsed, resulting in two deaths and several people feared swept away. This incident highlights India's crumbling infrastructure. A 2024 report revealed 21 bridge collapses on National Highways in the past three years. A 2020 study found 2,130 bridge collapses in India over 40 years, primarily attributed to natural disasters (80.3%), material deterioration (10.1%), and overloading (3.28%). Recent major bridge collapses include the Morbi Suspension Bridge (2022), which killed 135 people, the Mumbai foot overbridge (2019), resulting in six deaths, and the Varanasi flyover (2018), which caused under-construction worker fatalities.
