Varanasi's Dairy Daughter Scores for India

2025-04-22T02:44:00+00:00

Pooja Yadav, 20, from Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, has become the first woman from her city to play for the Indian women's hockey team. Her father, Mahendra, a milkman and farmer, supported her dream despite skepticism from villagers. He bought her first hockey shoes, paying for them with milk deliveries. Pooja's passion for sports began with playing cricket with boys and later transitioned to hockey. Her mother, Kalavati, and sisters were her constant source of encouragement. Pooja secured a spot at the Lucknow sports hostel to focus on training, alleviating the financial burden on her family.
