"Jail Inmates Receive Medical Aid: Red Cross Brings Health and Hope Behind Bars"

2025-02-23T18:32:00+00:00

The Indian Red Cross Society, Gorakhpur branch, organized a health camp at Gorakhpur district jail. Led by chairman Shivendra Vikram Singh, the camp was supported by jail superintendent J.K. Pandey. A total of 120 inmates underwent skin disease screening and 110 received medicines. A gynecologist, Dr Pooja Shukla, conducted health check-ups for female inmates. Additionally, doctors from Raji Hospital examined the eyesight of 133 inmates and distributed glasses to 30. The initiative was praised by SP DK Pandey, who assured continued support for such programs. The health camp provided essential medical services to the inmates, addressing their skin and eye health concerns. The event demonstrated the collaborative efforts of the Indian Red Cross Society and the jail authorities to improve the well-being of the inmates.
