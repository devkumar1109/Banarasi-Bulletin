Varanasi's Giant Brinjal: A GI-Tagged Treasure

2025-05-27T12:50:00+00:00

Ramnagar Bhanta, a GI-tagged brinjal variety from Varanasi, is celebrated for its large size, rich flavor, and cultural significance. Recognized in 2023, the GI tag supports traditional farming practices and enhances local cuisine. Cultivated in Varanasi, Mirzapur, Chandauli, and Sonbhadra, this winter vegetable is prized for its unique taste, exceptional size (2 to 2.5 kilograms), and nutritional benefits. The brinjal transitions from greenish-white to golden yellow as it matures, signaling its readiness for seed extraction, a key aspect of traditional farming. Ramnagar Bhanta is a culinary staple in North Indian dishes like bati-chokha and is renowned for its creamy texture and slightly smoky flavor.
