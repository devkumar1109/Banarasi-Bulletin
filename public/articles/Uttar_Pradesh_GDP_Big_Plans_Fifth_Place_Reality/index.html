Uttar Pradesh GDP: Big Plans, Fifth Place Reality

2025-04-25T06:57:00+00:00

Despite claims of being the second-largest economy, Uttar Pradesh currently ranks fifth in projected GDP for 2024-25, trailing Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and Gujarat. The state government, led by Yogi Adityanath, aims to achieve a trillion-dollar economy by 2029. Since 2018, they have received ₹45 lakh crore in investment proposals, with ₹15 lakh crore already implemented, creating 60 lakh jobs and an unemployment rate of 2.4%. To achieve this goal, they are organizing another Investors' Summit with a target of attracting ₹33 lakh crore, aiming for ₹100 lakh crore in investments by 2027. They are also focusing on digitizing land use changes and tackling regional imbalances through initiatives like the Bundelkhand Industrial Development Authority, which aims to develop 56,000 acres with ₹5,000 crore allocation.
