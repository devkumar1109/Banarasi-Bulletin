U.P. Chips In: Semiconductors Fuel State's Growth

2025-05-23T13:01:00+00:00

Uttar Pradesh is rapidly becoming a key player in India's semiconductor sector with investments of ₹30,000 crore. Two major projects, a ₹3,706 crore joint venture between HCL and Foxconn near Jewar airport focusing on display driver chips, and a proposed ₹27,000 crore investment by Tarq Semiconductors (Hiranandani Group) in Noida for integrated circuit production, are driving this growth. Launched 17 months ago, Uttar Pradesh's dedicated Semiconductor Policy offers up to 50% capital subsidy, 75% land discount, and 100% stamp duty exemption, attracting global investors. The state already contributes significantly to India's mobile phone and component manufacturing, further strengthening its semiconductor ambitions. Chief Secretary Manoj Kumar Singh emphasizes Uttar Pradesh's aim to lead the semiconductor landscape, aligning with the vision of Digital India and Aatmanirbhar Bharat.
