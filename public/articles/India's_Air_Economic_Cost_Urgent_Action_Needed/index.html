India's Air: Economic Cost, Urgent Action Needed

2025-03-28T07:30:00+00:00

Air pollution in India is a pressing economic issue alongside its health implications. Despite ₹858 crore allocated by the central government for pollution control in 2024-25 remaining unspent, Delhi's 2025-26 budget allocates ₹300 crore for emergency pollution control measures and a new monitoring center. The article highlights that pollution impacts economic activity and workforce productivity. Addressing this requires a coordinated multi-sectoral response. While the urgency is recognized, implementation often falls short. India aims to tackle this crisis by 2047, but decisive action is needed.
