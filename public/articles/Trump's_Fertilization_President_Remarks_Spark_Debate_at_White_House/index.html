Trump's "Fertilization President" Remarks Spark Debate at White House

2025-03-27T05:00:00+00:00

During a Women's History Month event at the White House, former President Donald Trump sparked controversy with comments like calling himself the "Fertilisation President" while discussing IVF access. He also promised "tremendous goodies" for women. Trump honored historical figures like Betsy Ross and Harriet Tubman, praising their contributions to the nation's greatness. He acknowledged women making history today, citing Suzy Wiles and highlighting his administration's record number of women in the Cabinet. Trump criticized past administrations for their approach to womanhood and declared an end to what he called a "war on women," emphasizing his administration's focus on protecting women's rights and dignity. He touted economic achievements, including lower costs for eggs and energy, and praised Linda McMahon's work on education. Trump expressed concern over rising autism rates and announced a Presidential Commission to Make America Healthy Again. He also signed an order expanding access to fertility treatments like IVF.
