Varanasi Fights TB: 6 Lakh Screened for World TB Day

2025-03-24T17:58:00+00:00

To mark World TB Day, over 6 lakh people were screened for tuberculosis in Varanasi district. The '100-day Intensive TB Elimination Campaign' launched by the Government of India on December 7, 2024, aimed to quickly identify and treat TB cases. As part of the campaign, 9,781 people underwent the NAT test. TB is an infectious disease primarily affecting the lungs, caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It spreads through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. Untreated TB can spread to other parts of the body through the blood or lymphatic system. Besides the lungs, TB can affect the brain, bones and joints, kidneys, intestines, and heart. 310 nutrition kits were distributed to TB patients at various medical units in Varanasi by Feeding India.
