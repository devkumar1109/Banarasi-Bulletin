Varanasi Sewai: Eid Tradition, Cultural Harmony

2025-03-29T19:22:00+00:00

In Varanasi, Hindu artisans have a centuries-old tradition of manufacturing sewai (vermicelli) that is highly sought after during Muslim Eid celebrations. The premium ‘kimami' sewai, both raw and white, is a popular choice during Eid. This industry, started as a cottage industry decades ago, now employs around 2,000 people in Varanasi. Production intensifies in the months leading up to Ramzan, with exports fulfilling international demand. Varanasi's sewai is known for its thinness, special texture achieved with refined flour and ghee, and meticulous cleanliness. While sales are good year-round, Eid brings the peak season for this unique culinary tradition that exemplifies cultural integration.
