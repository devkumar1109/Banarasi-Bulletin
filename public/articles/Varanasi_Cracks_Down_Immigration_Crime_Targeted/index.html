Varanasi Cracks Down: Immigration, Crime Targeted

2025-04-30T19:34:00+00:00

The Varanasi police have launched a drive to identify and remove illegal Bangladeshi and Rohingya immigrants. Commissioner Mohit Agarwal ordered increased vigilance at public places like railway stations, temples, and ghats. He instructed officials to verify individuals residing on roadsides and in parks, urging landlords to conduct tenant checks. Agarwal emphasized CCTV surveillance in rural areas and sought the installation of cameras in villages. He also directed a crackdown on drug peddling, cattle smuggling, and illegal arms trafficking. Online gambling networks will be targeted, with potential action under the Gangster Act. Police stations are to conduct surprise checks at accommodations and increase night patrols. 'Operation Chakravyuh' will involve special checking drives at strategic locations.
