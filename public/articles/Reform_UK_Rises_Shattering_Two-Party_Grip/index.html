Reform UK Rises, Shattering Two-Party Grip

2025-05-04T07:00:00+00:00

Reform UK, led by Nigel Farage, achieved a historic victory in the 2025 local elections, securing 677 council seats and control of 10 councils. This success surpassed both Labour and the Conservatives, challenging the traditional two-party system. Reform UK also won a parliamentary by-election in Runcorn and Helsby. Their rise is attributed to public dissatisfaction with immigration, economic policies, and governance, reflecting a growing populist trend. The party's focus on key issues has resonated with voters, indicating a shift in political preferences towards alternatives to established parties.
