Nehru: Unity in Diversity, Through Language

2025-03-01T04:20:00+00:00

Jawaharlal Nehru, India's first prime minister, navigated complex linguistic challenges in post-Independence India. Robert D. King's book, "Nehru and the Language Politics of India," highlights Nehru's nuanced approach to language, particularly his support for Urdu. Nehru recognized the interconnectedness of Hindi and Urdu, emphasizing their mutual strength and the harm in suppressing one language for the benefit of another. He viewed Urdu as a symbol of Hindu-Muslim unity and a secular India. Despite facing criticism from Hindi zealots, Nehru championed a cosmopolitan vision where language diversity was celebrated. His perspective on language politics, a reflection of his commitment to national unity, remains relevant even amidst contemporary political attacks on the Nehruvian paradigm.
