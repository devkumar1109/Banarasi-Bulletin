India Sees COVID Surge Fueled by Omicron Subvariants

2025-06-02T10:52:00+00:00

India is experiencing a surge in COVID-19 cases. As of June 2nd, there are 3,961 active cases, a significant jump from 257 the previous day. 685 new cases were reported, mostly managed through home care. Kerala has the highest number of active cases (1,336), followed by Maharashtra (467). Other states like Delhi, Gujarat, Karnataka, West Bengal, Tamil Nadu, and Uttar Pradesh also report significant numbers. The Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) attributes the rise to Omicron subvariants like LF.7, XFG, JN.1, and NB.1.8.1. While these subvariants are prevalent, official sources note that infections remain relatively mild.
