Modi Fuels Bihar's Growth with ₹48,500 Cr Projects

2025-05-30T18:41:00+00:00

Prime Minister Narendra Modi launched development projects worth over ₹48,500 crore in Bihar on Friday, May 30th. A key project is the Nabinagar Super Thermal Power Project, Stage-II (3x800 MW) in Aurangabad district, costing over ₹29,930 crore. This project aims to enhance energy security for Bihar and eastern India, stimulate industrial growth, create jobs, and provide affordable electricity. Additionally, PM Modi laid the foundation stone for various road infrastructure projects, including the four-laning of the Patna–Arrah–Sasaram section of NH-119A, the six-laning of the Varanasi–Ranchi–Kolkata highway (NH-319B) and Ramnagar–Kacchi Dargah stretch (NH-119D), and the construction of a new Ganga bridge between Buxar and Bharauli.
