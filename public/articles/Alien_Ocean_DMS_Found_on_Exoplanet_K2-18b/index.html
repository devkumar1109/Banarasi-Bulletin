Alien Ocean: DMS Found on Exoplanet K2-18b

2025-04-18T08:37:00+00:00

Dr. Nikku Madhusudhan, an Indian-origin astrophysicist at the University of Cambridge, and his team have detected dimethyl sulfide (DMS) in the atmosphere of K2-18b, an exoplanet 120 light-years from Earth. DMS is a biosignature found exclusively on Earth, produced by living organisms, primarily marine algae. This discovery, using data from the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST), strengthens the hypothesis that K2-18b, a "sub-Neptune" planet, could support life. While not confirming life, the detection of DMS, alongside methane and carbon dioxide, suggests K2-18b may have a warm ocean teeming with life. This finding is a significant step in the search for extraterrestrial life and addresses the Fermi Paradox, the contradiction between the high probability of extraterrestrial life and the lack of evidence.
