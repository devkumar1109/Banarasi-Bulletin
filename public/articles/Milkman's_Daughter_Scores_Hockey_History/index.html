Milkman's Daughter Scores Hockey History

2025-04-22T02:44:00+00:00

Pooja Yadav, 20, from Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, has become the first woman from her city to play for the Indian women's hockey team. Her journey began with a pair of golden-starred playing shoes bought by her father, a milkman, who paid for them by delivering milk to the local shop owner. Pooja's early exposure to cricket with boys instilled a belief in her equality and paved the way for her hockey training. Despite initial doubts from villagers, Pooja's mother and sisters remained her biggest supporters, encouraging her to pursue her dream even after facing setbacks. Pooja's selection for the national team is a source of immense pride for her family and community, inspiring other girls to consider hockey as a career path.
